About Eridian numerals

Eridians use a base-6 number system. Where humans count 0, 1, 2 … 9, Eridians count ℓ I V λ + Ɐ and roll over, the same way decimal rolls at 10.

This page also includes base conversions. If you'd like to do math in base-6, convert the numbers to base-10, perform the operations, and then convert the final result backto base-6.

About Eridian time

The planet Erid rotates fast about its axis, and orbits much closer to 40 Eridani A than Earth orbits the sun. As such, days and years on Erid are shorter than ours.

An Eridian solar day is 18,360 Earth seconds, or ~5.1 hours. An Eridian solar year is 199 Erid days or ~42.25 Earth days. The Eridian second is 2.366 Earth seconds long.

Eridians cleanly divide their day into Iℓℓℓℓℓ (7,776) erid-seconds. These provide close analogues to Earth units of time: there are Iℓ (6) erid-hours in a Eridian day, Iℓℓ (36) centihours per erid-hour, and Iℓℓ (36) erid-seconds per centihour. An erid-hour ≈ 51 Earth minutes; a centihour ≈ 85 Earth seconds.

Because each subdivision is a power of 6, the clock digits concatenate directly into total erid-seconds. Reading H:CC:SS left to right gives the 5-digit base-6 total — for example, V:Iλ:Vℓ (V erid-hr + Iλ centihrs + Vℓ erid-secs) = VIλVℓ total erid-seconds.

About Eridian weight

Erid's gravity is approximately 2.09× Earth's, so you'd weigh about twice as much on the surface. Unfortunately, there are no canonical Eridian weight units, so these are all in Earth units.
